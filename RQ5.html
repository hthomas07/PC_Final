Research Question 1

How did public transit ridership in New York change over time, particularly before and after the COVID-19 pandemic?

Visualization

Insights

1. Peak Ridership in Summer Months: Ridership consistently spikes during the summer months, particularly in July, August, and September. This pattern aligns with increased recreational activities, favorable weather conditions, and tourism, which drive more people to use ferries.

2. Significant Decline in Winter Months: Ridership drops sharply during the colder months, such as November, December, and January, likely due to harsher weather conditions and a reduction in outdoor and tourist-related activities.

3. Gradual Transition in Spring and Fall: Ridership begins to increase in the spring (April-May) as weather improves and declines in the fall (October-November) as temperatures drop, reflecting a clear seasonal progression in ferry usage.
