Research Question 4

How did public transit ridership in New York change over time, particularly before and after the COVID-19 pandemic?

Visualization

Insights

1. Subway Vandalism Dominates Across All Months: Subway systems consistently report significantly higher incidents of vandalism compared to buses, indicating they are the primary target for such activities, likely due to higher ridership and system exposure.

2. Sharp Spike in Early 2022: Vandalism incidents reached a peak in early 2022, surpassing 150 incidents. This could be linked to increased system usage post-pandemic restrictions, leading to higher exposure and activity in transit systems.

3. Consistent Low Bus Vandalism Levels: Bus vandalism remains relatively low throughout the observed period, contributing only a small fraction to the overall incidents. This might reflect factors like lower visibility or security measures on bus routes compared to subway stations.

4. Seasonal Patterns in Subway Vandalism: There appear to be recurring mid-year (summer) dips in subway vandalism incidents, potentially due to reduced commuter traffic or increased outdoor activities during warmer months.
